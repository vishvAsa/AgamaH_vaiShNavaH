This book is the result of many years of effort, and I would like to thank all those who generously helped it to come to fruition. The book began as a DPhil thesis at the University of Oxford, I would like to thank Prof. Gavin Flood, who accepted my research proposal and provided encouragement and direction throughout my doctorate. I also wish to thank Dr Rembert Lutjeharms who was a constant source of support, he provided detailed suggestions after carefully reading through various drafts of this work and helped with challenging Sanskrit passages. I also wish to thank Prof. Faisal Devji, Prof. Tony Stewart, Prof. Diwakar Acharya and Dr Kiyokazu Okita, for meticulously reading through the drafts of my work and providing valuable recommendations at vital stages. I am also grateful to Shaunaka Rishi Das, the Director of the Oxford Centre for Hindu Studies for his encouragement and for introducing a facility to nurture such research pursuits.

During my research trips to India many helped to develop this project. I wish to thank Dr Giles Tillotson, the Consultant Director at the Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II Museum in Jaipur who helped to obtain permission to access vital Sanskrit manuscripts.

I would also like to thank Prof. Ganesh U.Tithe from the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute and Nityānanda Artjoms Hvans from the Vṛndavāna institute who both helped to decipher the content of these Sanskrit manuscripts. I also wish to thank my family without whom this endeavour would not have been possible. My father, and mother, who supported me in every possible way and my wife who tirelessly supported me and selflessly sacrificed throughout. Finally, I wish to offer my respects to my spiritual teacher, Radhanath Swami, who inspired me to embark on this journey and to whom I dedicate this book.
