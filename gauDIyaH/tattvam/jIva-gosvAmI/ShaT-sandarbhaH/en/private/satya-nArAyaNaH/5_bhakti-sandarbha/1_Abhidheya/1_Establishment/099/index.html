Bhakti Contrasted with Karma ९९ । अथ व्यतिरेकेण कर्मानादरेणाह - तत्र कर्मणः फलप्राप्तावनिश्चयवत्त्वं दुःखरूपत्वं च भक्तेस्तु तस्यामावश्यकत्वं साधकदशायामपि सुखरूपत्वं चेत्याह ( भा० १११८ ११२ ) - NOW BHAKTI is established as the abhidheya through negative concomitance (vyatireka), by expressing disregard for the path of karma. There is both uncertainty and misery involved in attaining the results of karma. But in the case of bhakti, success is assured and the devotee experiences joy even in the stage of practice. These conclusions are summed up in the following statement of the sage Saunaka to Sūta Gosvāmi: कर्मण्यस्मिन्ननाश्वासे धूमधूम्रात्मनां भवान् । आपाययति गोविन्दपादपद्मासवं मधु || १७९ ।। We have been engaged in the performance of this sacrifice, the fruits of which are uncertain, until our bodies were blackened by smoke. Now you have come and revived us by causing us to drink the ambrosial nectar flowing from Śrī Govinda’s lotus feet. (SB 1.18.12)1 अस्मिन् कर्मणि सत्रेऽनाश्वासेऽविश्वसनीये वैगुण्यबाहुल्येन कृषिवत् फलनिश्चयाभावात् । अनेन भक्तेर्विश्वसनीयत्वं ध्वनितम् । धूमेन धूम्रो विरञ्जितौ विवर्णावात्मानौ शरीरचित्ते येषाम् । कर्मणि षष्ठी तानस्मानित्यर्थः । पादपद्मस्य यशोरूपमासर्व मकरन्द मधु मधुरम् । अत्र सत्रवत् कर्मान्तरं यशःश्रवणवद् भक्त्यन्तरं चेति ज्ञेयम् । तदेवं भक्तिविनाभूतानां कर्मादिभिरस्माकं दुःखमेवासीदिति व्यतिरेकत्वमत्र गम्यते । 1 | karmany asminn anāśväse dhuma-dhumratmanām bhavan apayayati govinda-pada-padmāsavam madhu 507 I Bhakti Is the Complete Abhidheya In this endeavor (asmin karmani), meaning “in this sacrifice” (asmin satre), the outcome is “unassured” (anāśvāse), or “unreliable” (aviśvasaniya). Because of the possibility of many discrepancies (vaigunya-bahulyena) in the performance of sacrifice, there is a want of certainty in regard to the result (phalaniścayābhāvāt), as is the case with farming. By this statement, the reliability (viśvasanīyatvam) of bhakti is indirectly implied. In the compound dhuma-dhumratmanām, the word ātmanām refers to “the body and mind” (sarira-citte), dhumra means “dispirited” (virañjita) and “discolored” (vivarna), and dhuma means “by the smoke” (dhūmena). Thus, the compound as a whole means “to us, who have become dispirited and discolored in mind and body due to the smoke.” Although ātmanām appears in the sixth case [the possessive case], it is used in the sense of the accusative, [indicating that the sages are the direct object of the verb āpāyayati, “You are causing us to drink"]. In the compound pada-padmāsavam, the word asavam means, “the intoxicating nectar of a flower” (makarandam), which flows from the lotus feet (pada-padma) of Śrī Govinda in the form of His fame (yaso-rupam). This nectar, or fame, is “honey-like” or “ambrosial” (madhu). In this statement, it is to be understood that the uncertainty and hardship that is specifically pointed out in regard to the satra sacrifice actually applies to all other forms of karma. Similarly, the certainty and pleasure attributed to hearing the glories of Bhagavan applies to all other types of bhakti. Thus, by negative concomitance (vyatireka), it is understood from Saunaka’s statement that by the execution of karma alone devoid of bhakti, the performers experienced only misery. तदुक्तम् “यशः श्रियामेव परिश्रमः परः " ( भा०१२ | १२ | ५३ ) इत्यादि “ अतो वै कवयो नित्यम् " ( भा० १।२।२२ ) इत्यादि च । This same idea was expressed by Sūta Gosvāmī: The immense labor expended in discharging prescribed duties pertaining to one’s varna and aśrama, and in undergoing 508 99 Bhakti Contrasted with Karma penance, study of the Vedas, and other similar practices, culminates only in wealth accompanied by fame. But by hearing and repeating the glories of Sri Hari, one attains unbroken remembrance of the lotus feet of Bhagavan, who is the support of the Goddess of Fortune. (SB 12.12.53)2 And: For this reason indeed, the enlightened sages, out of supreme delight, continuously engage in bhakti to Bhagavan Vasudeva, which is utterly fulfilling to the self. (SB 1.2.22)3 ब्रह्मवैवर्ते च शिवं प्रति श्रीविष्णुवाक्यम्- In the Brahma-vaivarta Purana as well, Bhagavan Śrī Viṣṇu makes a similar statement to Śiva: यदि मां प्राप्तुमिच्छन्ति प्राप्नुवन्त्येव नान्यथा । कलौ कलुषचित्तानां वृथायुः प्रभृतीनि च । भवन्ति वर्णाश्रमिणां न तु मच्छरणार्थिनाम् ॥ १८० ॥ इति ॥ वृषयः सूतम् ॥ If anyone seeks to attain Me, they certainly do attain Me. This cannot be otherwise. In the age of Kali, the life and other characteristics of the impure-hearted who adhere to varṇāśrama are all futile, but this is not the case for those who have taken shelter of Me. (Brahma-vaivarta Purana)* Commentary AFTER demonstrating through positive affirmations (anvaya) that bhakti is independent in awarding its result and that other paths 2 yaśaḥ-śriyam eva pariśramah paro varṇāśramācāra-tapaḥ-śrutādiṣu 3 4 avismṛtiḥ śridhara-pada-padmayor guṇanuvada-śravaṇādirbhir hareḥ ato vai kavayo nityam bhaktim paramaya muda vasudeve bhagavati kurvanty atma-prasādanim yadi mam präptum icchanti präpnuvanty eva nanyathā kalau kaluṣa-cittānām vṛthāyuh prabhṛtini ca bhavanti varnasraminam na tu mac-charaṇārthinam 509 I Bhakti Is the Complete Abhidheya depend upon it, Śrī Jīva now establishes the same conclusion through disaffirmation (vyatireka), by showing the inefficacy of other paths. In this anuccheda, he shows the futility of karma devoid of bhakti. Thousands of sages headed by Saunaka had assembled at Naimiṣāranya to participate in a long-enduring yajña, specified by the name satra. According to Vyasa’s statement in the beginning of Srimad Bhāgavata, it was meant to last for a thousand years (SB 1.1.4). The sages, however, were unsure that they would obtain the desired result. On this account, they referred to the sacrifice by the word anāśvāse, or “uncertain.” They also found the whole endeavor to be quite troublesome, and thus they admitted that their minds and bodies had become covered in soot from the smoke of the sacrificial fire. It is understandable that their bodies became covered in soot, but how could such a thing be said of their minds? This indicates that they were feeling mental dissatisfaction and uncertainty in regard to the result. In this connection, Śrī Jīva glosses the word dhümra, lit., “smoke-colored,” with the two words. viranjita and vivarna. Although both of these words mean “discolored,” the first word in particular can also carry the sense of “dispirited” or “disheartened,” which is perhaps what Śrī Jiva had in mind in supplying two otherwise perfectly synonymous terms. The path of sacrificial action (karma-marga) is so precise that if the ritual is not executed exactly as prescribed down to the last detail, it will not yield the desired result. A slight deviation even in the intonation of a single phoneme in a mantra may even bring to bear the exact opposite of what was intended. The story of the demon Vṛtrāsura, who was generated from a dark tantrika ritual, and that of Diti, who contrived to conceive a son who would be able to kill Indra, are glaring examples of the fallibility of karma. In contrast, bhakti is pleasurable even during the stage of practice, and its result is assured because of the supreme independent power with which it is intrinsically endowed. If there is any imperfection in the course of the performance of bhakti, Bhagavan personally brings about its completion, as confirmed by the sage Karabhājana: 510 99 Bhakti Contrasted with Karma If a beloved devotee of Bhagavan, who is engaged in rendering service to the soles of His feet, having given up all other objects of affection, somehow becomes involved in a forbidden act, then the Supreme Master Hari, situated in the devotee’s heart, destroys all reactions to such an act.
