Bhakti Is the Source of All Perfection ९७ । यान्यन्यानि सर्वाणि तत्र पुरुषार्थसाधनान्युच्यन्ते तान्यपि तथैव भक्तिमूलान्येव । यथा ( भा० १० । ८१ ।१९ ) - ALL other practices (sādhanas) that are described in Śrīmad Bhāgavata as productive of the ultimate good (puruṣārtha) are similarly rooted only in bhakti, as expressed in these words of Śrīdāmā Vipra: स्वर्गापवर्गयोः पुंसां रसायां भुवि सम्पदाम् । सर्वासामपि सिद्धीनां मूलं तच्चरणार्चनम् || १७१ || Worship of Your lotus feet is the root of all perfections and opulences that human beings can attain in heaven, in liberation, on earth, or in the subterrestrial realms. (SB 10.81.19 ) 1 “मन्त्रतस्तन्त्रतश्छिद्रम्” (भा०८।२३।१६ ) इत्यादिन्यायेन “मुखबाहूरुपादेभ्यः " ( भा० ११।५।२) इत्याद्युक्तनित्यत्वेन च सर्वथा तद्बहिर्मुखानां तु तत्तदलाभ एव स्यादित्यर्थः । यथा स्कान्दे- 1 [In undertaking the ritual process associated with motivated action (kāmya-karma), many errors can arise in regard to mantra and sadhana.] In this respect, the following principle is enunciated [about bhakti]: svargāpavargayoḥ pumsām rasayam bhuvi sampadam sarvāsām api siddhīnām mūlam tac-caraṇārcanam 493 I Bhakti Is the Complete Abhidheya Defects pertaining to the pronunciation of mantras, the procedure to be followed, the time and location of the ritual, the eligibility of the persons involved, and the items employed are all made flawless by chanting Your names. (SB 8.23.16)2 Additionally, bhakti’s perpetuity (nityatva), meaning, its regular compulsory nature, is expressed in this pair of verses: From the mouth, arms, thighs, and feet of the Purusa appeared the four respective vocational classifications (varnas) along with the four stages of life (asramas). The varnas were distinctively divided into vipra, ksatriya, vaisya, and sūdra according to the corresponding gunas that determine their natures. Among human beings belonging to these divisions, those who [naively] neglect to worship the Purușa, who is directly the primordial ground of being of all individual beings (atmaprabhavam) and the Supreme Immanent Regulator of all being (Iśvara), or who [knowingly] show Him contempt, are deprived of their status and fall into degradation. (SB 11.5.2-3)3 Thus, by virtue of the principle that bhakti rectifies all defects as well as by its necessity (nityatva) - shown respectively in the above two verses - it is understood that those whose regard is directed away from Bhagavan (tad-bahirmukhānām), certainly cannot attain their respective aspired for goals, as stated in Skanda Purāṇa: 2 3 4 विष्णुभक्तिविहीनानां श्रौताः स्मार्ताश्च याः क्रियाः । कायक्लेशः फलं तासां स्वैरिणीव्यभिचारवत् ॥ १७२ ॥ इति । The actions performed according to Śruti and Smrti of those who are devoid of bhakti to Vişņu result only in bodily misery, like the illicit affairs of a prostitute. And if they should so desire, they can attain even material boons. Others, however, cannot attain such things. (SB 10.72.4)5 अत उक्तं बृहन्नारदीये - Therefore, it is said in the Bṛhan-nāradīya Purāṇa: यथा समस्तलोकानां जीवनं सलिलं स्मृतम् । तथा समस्तसिद्धीनां जीवनं भक्तिरिष्यते ॥ १७४ ॥ इति ॥ श्रीदामविप्रः ॥ Just as water is said to be the life of all living beings, so too bhakti is acknowledged as the life of all perfections. (BNP 1.4.4) Commentary BHAKTI is the basis of success in all undertakings, be it karmayoga, jñāna-yoga, or aṣṭanga-yoga. Even in worldly dealings, people attain success by polite and humble behavior, not by arrogance or indifference. Even politicians have to make a show of devotion and promise public service to win votes. If even material success is reliant on a devotional disposition, how much more must this be the case in regard to spiritual endeavors? Bhagavan is the Supreme Master and Regulator of everything. We have the capacity to act, but the result is not in our control. This is clearly expressed by Sri Krsna in the Gita: “Your eligibility pertains exclusively to the fulfillment of action but never to the fruits thereof” (GITA 2.47)? There are many factors involved in the outcome of actions, such as time and place. Beyond all other material considerations, there is the overseership of the Supreme Immanent Self, Paramātmā. Everything functions under His supervision. Therefore, no result can come about without His consent. Sudama thus concludes that all perfections are rooted in the worship of Kṛṣṇa’s feet (SB 10.81.19). Śrī Jiva then cites three verses to show that bhakti is a compulsory duty, without which all other prescribed duties are flawed. The first verse (SB 8.23.16) is spoken by Sukrācārya, the guru of Bali, who was the king of the asuras. Bali’s yajña was interrupted by Vamanadeva when He went there to beg land from Bali. Bhagavan Vamanadeva had requested Sukracārya to complete any deficiency that might occur in the yajña. In response to this proposal, Šukrācārya spoke this verse. Sukrācārya declared that all perfection comes about merely by chanting the name of Bhagavan. Those seeking material rewards alone can obtain the results of their respective undertakings by the grace of bhakti, but if one performs the same actions as an offering to Bhagavan, one will also attain bhakti in course of time. The next pair of verses cited by Śrī Jiva (SB 11.5.2-3) is spoken by the sage Camasa. He makes it emphatically clear that without devotion to the Supreme Self, who is the very source of all being, nobody can succeed in any endeavor. Indeed, even if one has attained a high position, he will lose it. This is further confirmed by King Yudhisthira (SB 10.72.4).
