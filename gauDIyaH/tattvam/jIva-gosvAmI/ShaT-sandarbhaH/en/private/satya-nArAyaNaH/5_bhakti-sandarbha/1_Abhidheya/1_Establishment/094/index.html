Bhakti Is the Fruit of All Scriptural Study ९४ । तत्रैवान्वयेन सर्वशास्त्रश्रवणफलत्वं सकैमुत्यमाह (भा० ३ | १३ |४) - AGAIN, by positive concomitance, Śrī Vidura establishes bhakti as the fruit of hearing, or studying, all scriptures with reference to the a fortiori argument (kaimutya) [lit., the principle of “how much more”]:’ श्रुतस्य पुंसां सुचिरश्रमस्य नन्वञ्जसा सूरिभिरीडितोऽर्थः । तत्तद्गुणानुश्रवणं मुकुन्दपादारविन्दं हृदयेषु येषाम् || १६२ || To repeatedly hear the virtues of those in whose hearts the lotus feet of Mukunda perpetually reside has been rightly esteemed by the wise as the true aim of the extensive effort undertaken by human beings in studying the scriptures. (SB 3.13.4) 2 पुंसां श्रुतस्य वेदार्थावगतेरयमेवार्थः प्रयोजनमीडितः श्लाघितः । कोऽसौ ? मुकुन्दस्य पादारविन्दं येषां हृदयेषु वर्तते तेषां तत्तगुणानां भगवद्भक्त्यात्मकानामनुश्रवणं यत् सोऽयमिति । ततः सुतरामेव श्रीमुकुन्दस्येत्यर्थः । एवमेवोक्तम् “वासुदेवपरा वेदाः " (भा० ११२।२८) इत्यादि । “भगवान् ब्रह्म कात्स्न्र्त्स्न्येन” (भा० २१२।३४) इत्यादि । 1 2 Kaimutya-nyaya is a type of logic used to establish a point of far-reaching significance by comparison with a truth of lesser significance, i.e., “If that is true, how much more true must this be?” In the verse quoted above, simply hearing about the virtues of those who keep Bhagavan Mukunda in their hearts is said to be the true aim of studying the scriptures. If this is the case, how much more beneficial must it be to directly hear about the virtues of Bhagavan Himself? Thus, bhakti to Bhagavan is established here as the genuine purpose of studying the scriptures through the kaimutya principle. śrutasya pumsam sucira-sramasya nany anjasa suribhir idito’rthah tat-tad-gunanusravanam mukunda-padaravindam hṛdayeşu yeşăm 479 1 Bhakti Is the Complete Abhidheya

This alone (ayam eva) has been esteemed (iditaḥ, i.e., slaghitaḥ) as the true aim (arthaḥ), meaning the genuine purpose (prayojanam), of scriptural study (śrutasya), or in other words, of the effort to ascertain the meaning of the Vedas (veda-arthaavagateḥ), undertaken by human beings (pumsăm). What has been thus esteemed? To repeatedly hear about the distinct virtues - one in essential nature with bhakti to Bhagavan – of those in whose hearts the lotus feet of Mukunda perpetually reside. From this statement, it is all the more self-evident that hearing directly about the virtues of Śrī Mukunda is certainly esteemed as the genuine purpose of studying the Vedas. This indeed was stated earlier - vasudeva-parā vedāḥ, “The Vedas have as their exclusive aim Śrī Vasudeva” (SB 1.2.28). This is further confirmed in the following verse as well: After thrice studying and deliberating upon the entire corpus of the Vedas, the venerable Brahma, who was of one-pointed intellectual resolve, ascertained through his faculty of discernment that by which love for the Self [Śri Hari] is evoked. (SB 2.2.34)3 तथा च पाद्मबृहत्सहस्रनाम्नि - The same conclusion is expressed in the Bṛhat-sahasra-nāmastotra found in the Padma Purāṇa: स्मर्तव्यः सततं विष्णुर्विस्मर्तव्यो न जातुचित् । सर्वे विधिनिषेधाः स्युरेतयोरेव किङ्कराः ॥ १६३ ॥ One should remember Bhagavan Viṣṇu always and never forget Him even for a moment. All the rules and prohibitions of scripture are servants of these two principles alone. (Padma Purana, Uttara-khanda 71.100)* तथा च स्कान्दे प्रभासखण्डे लिङ्गपुराणे च- 3 bhagavan brahma kartsnyena trir anviksya maniṣayā tad-adhyavasyat kuṭastho ratir atman yato bhavet smartavyaḥ satatam visnur vismartavyo na jātucit sarve vidhi-niṣedhah syur etayor eva kinkarāḥ 480 94 Bhakti Is the Fruit of All Scriptural Study In the Prabhasa-khanda of the Skanda Purāņa and in the Linga Purana also, it is said: आलोड्य सर्वशास्त्राणि विचार्य च पुनः पुनः । इदमेव सुनिष्पन्नं ध्येयो नारायणः सदा ॥ १६४ ॥ After churning all the scriptures and deliberating on them again and again, this conclusion alone has clearly come to the fore-that one should continuously meditate on Bhagavan Nārāyaṇa. (Skanda Purana 4.25.35, Linga Purana 2.7.11)5 अत एव वेदार्पणमन्त्र - Therefore, in the mantra that is offered to undertake the study of the Vedas, it is said: इति विद्यातपोयोनिरयोनिविष्णुरीडितः । ब्रह्मज्ञस्तपते देवः प्रीयतां मे जनार्दनः || १६५ || श्रीविदुरः || Thus, Bhagavan Viṣṇu is praised as the sourceless ground of all knowledge and penance. May the worshipful Bhagavan Janardana, for whom the knowers of the Vedas undergo penance, be pleased with me." Commentary IN THIS ANUCCHEDA, Śrī Jiva Gosvāmī demonstrates that it is not only Śrīmad Bhagavata that recommends bhakti as the abhidheya but that all scriptures do so. In establishing this conclusion, he first supplies evidence from Srimad Bhagavata and then from other Purāņas. The fact that all scriptures advocate bhakti certainly 2 aloḍya sarva-sastrani vicarya ca punaḥ punah idam eva sunispannam dhyeyo narayaṇaḥ sadā This verse is also found in Garuda Purana (229.1). iti vidya-tapo-yonir ayoni vişnur iditah brahma-jñas tapate devaḥ priyataṁ me janardanaḥ Source unknown 481482 I Bhakti Is the Complete Abhidheya stands to reason, because Bhagavan is the source of all scriptures, and He manifested them simply to disclose the means by which He Himself can be known to humanity. The root cause of the jivas’ suffering is their beginningless ignorance of Bhagavan. Consequently, the sole remedy for this condition is the knowledge and immediate awareness of Bhagavan. The scriptures are the authentic source of this knowledge, and so the means that they advocate - namely, bhakti - is the real purpose of all scriptures, of all paths, and of all rules and regulations. In the next anuccheda, Śrī Jīva Gosvāmi shows that even those scriptures or passages that propound varnasrama have bhakti alone as their ultimate purpose.
