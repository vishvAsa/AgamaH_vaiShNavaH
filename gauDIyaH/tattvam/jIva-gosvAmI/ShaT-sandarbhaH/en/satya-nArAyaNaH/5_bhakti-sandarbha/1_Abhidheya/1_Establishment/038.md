The Eyes and Legs Are Worthless without Bhakti 
१३८ । भा० २ । ३ ।२२- 
SŪTA GOSVĀMĪ continues: 
बर्हायिते ते नयने नराणां लिङ्गानि विष्णोर्न निरीक्षतो ये । पादौ नृणां तौ द्रुमजन्मभाजौ क्षेत्राणि नानुव्रजतो हरेर्यौ ॥ ५७ ॥ 
The eyes of human beings that do not behold the deity of Bhaga- vān Visņu are like the marks on the tail of a peacock. And the feet of men that do not journey to the holy places of Bhagavan Hari are as if born as trees. (SB 2.3.22)1 
जन्मभजेते इति तथा वृक्षमूलतुल्यावित्यर्थः ॥ 
The compound druma-janma-bhäjau means that such feet are as if born as trees, meaning that they are comparable to the roots of trees. 
Commentary 
THE COMPARISON of the eyes to the marks on the tail of a peacock signifies that they merely appear beautiful but do not function in the capacity for which they were designed. Instead of discerning
