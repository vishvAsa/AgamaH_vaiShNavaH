Commentary 
THE purpose of varṇāśrama is devotion to Śrī Krsna. It is a social system designed specifically to lead all of its members to the path of bhakti. A doubt may be raised in this regard. It has been estab- lished earlier that bhakti ensues only from bhakti itself. Such being the case, how can conventional moral acts, such as charity, give rise to bhakti? Śri Jiva Gosvāmī explains that the varṇāśrama principles can be practiced in two ways. They may be undertaken indepen- dent of Bhagavan, or they may be performed for His satisfaction. 
In the first case, if the varṇāśrama duties are meticulously per- formed, they will yield the aspired for material benefit. This too ultimately occurs only through the grace of bhakti, since the per- former must adhere to the scriptural injunctions, which are the orders of Bhagavan. On this account, Bhakti Devi rewards the per- former for their obedience to the divine ordinance. If, however, one carries out these prescribed acts without faith, and thus with- out authentic adherence to the requisite process, he or she will not obtain even the material result, as attested to by Śrī Kṛṣṇa:

O son of Prtha, whatever is offered in the sacrificial fire, whatever is given in charity, whatever austerity is undertaken, and what- ever other auspicious actions are performed without faith, are all termed asat, or worthless, because they bear no fruit, neither here nor after death. (GĪTĀ 17.28)' 
The alternate and superior manner of carrying out varṇāśrama duties is to do so for the satisfaction of Bhagavan. If conducted with this intent, then by the grace of a devotee, these sanctified acts will give rise to a taste for hearing about Kṛṣṇa. This in turn will impel the performer to take to pure bhakti. Only this type of execution of varṇāśrama principles is truly praiseworthy. This is the intended meaning of Uddhava's statement. 
In the next anuccheda, it will be demonstrated that true jñāna ensues only from bhakti.
