The book **Sat-Kriya-Sara-Dipika** (सत्-क्रिया-सार-दीपिका), often referred to simply as *Sat-Kriya-Dipika* or *Sat-Kriya-Deepa*, is traditionally attributed to **Śrīla Gopāla Bhaṭṭa Gosvāmī** (one of the Six Gosvamis of Vrindavan).

It is a *Vaishnava-smriti* (manual of codes and rituals) that provides detailed instructions for the performance of the ten *samskaras* (sacraments/rites of passage) and fire sacrifices specifically for the Gaudiya Vaishnava community.

## Controversies Regarding Authorship
While the Gaudiya Math and ISKCON lineages strongly uphold Gopāla Bhaṭṭa Gosvāmī as the author, there are several historical and scholarly controversies surrounding the text:

1.  **Stylistic Differences from Hari-bhakti-vilasa**:
    Gopāla Bhaṭṭa Gosvāmī is the primary author of the *Hari-bhakti-vilasa*, a massive compendium of Vaishnava law. Critics argue that the style, language, and "art of presentation" in *Sat-Kriya-Sara-Dipika* differ significantly from the *Hari-bhakti-vilasa*. This has led some scholars and members of other Gaudiya lineages to question if he actually wrote it.

2.  **Lack of Early Historical References**:
    Some critics point out that the text was not widely known or cited by major Gaudiya scholars in the centuries immediately following the Gosvami era. It became prominent primarily after it was edited and republished by **Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura** and **Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati Thakura** in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. This leads to theories that it might have been a later compilation attributed to the Gosvami to lend it authority.

3.  **The "Gopal Bhattaya Svaha" Oblation**:
    One specific point of internal controversy is that the text includes a *homa* (fire sacrifice) mantra: *"gopal bhattaya svaha"*. Skeptics argue it is unlikely a humble saint would include a mantra to himself in his own ritual manual, suggesting the book was either heavily edited by his followers or written by someone else in his honor.

4.  **Exclusivity of Use**:
    The book is almost exclusively used by the branches of the Gaudiya Math and ISKCON. Traditional *Babaji* or *Gosvami* lineages in Vrindavan and Bengal often follow other smriti manuals or traditional Brahmanical procedures, sometimes viewing *Sat-Kriya-Sara-Dipika* as a specialized or "sectarian" manual rather than a universal Gosvami text.

## Content Controversy
Beyond authorship, the content itself is sometimes seen as controversial because it **forbids the worship of Ganesha and other devatas** (demigods) during ceremonies, replacing them with the worship of Vishnu and His associates. This was a radical departure from the common *Smarta* Hindu practices of the time, which always begin with Ganesha worship. Defenders of the book argue this was necessary to establish the principle of *ananya-bhakti* (unalloyed devotion).
