## pAncharAtra list
Proto-avatArism of pA~ncharAtra had the following avatAra-s:
1. varAha
2. nR^isiMha
3. trivikrama --treta–
4. rAma jAmadagnya
5. rAma dAsharathi --dvApara–
6. kR^iShNa vAsudeva --kali–
7. nara-nArAyaNau --pralaya–
9. haMsa --new kR^ita–- veda-restoring vibhava-s
10. hayashiras


## trita's brothers
Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2044997028166418836)

We believe the account of avatAra-s given in the early pA~ncharAtra (mahAbharata 12.326) is one of the earliest reflexes of vaiShNava "avatArism" that had already incorporated rAma dAsharathi. Its early date is suggested by the fact that it has a para-rAmAyaNa mytheme that is mostly absent/forgotten in the rest of the rAmAyaNa tradition. 

It states that the first two Aptya-s of the veda (c.f. shatapatha brAhmaNa 1.2.3.1-2) had caused injury to the third, the famous trita (the well-known tale of them throwing trita into a well: already alluded to in the R^igveda: a cryptic statement of the value of pi being little over 3). However, here they are termed sons of prajApati. As a result of this affront to trita, they lost their form and became vAnara-s. They became the progenitors of several other monkeys, who together are prophesied to become the assistants of rAma in the killing of rAvaNa:
