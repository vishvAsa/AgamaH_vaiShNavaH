६९ थे फ़िवे वैष्णव ब्रह्मस् अरे अस् फ़ोल्लोwस् (स्वयम्भुवपञ्चरात्र, एx- पोसुरे १०अ१-२):

ओम् नरेणरेणराण्णाथ
नर यस्मान् नरोत्तम।
प्रथमब्रह्म ।

ओम् यज्ञाय नमो यानाय
धर्माय नमः पुण्याय नमः
व्रताय नमः । नियमाय नमः ।
मार्गानुसारिणे नमः
द्वितीयब्रह्म ।

ओम् कालेभ्यो ऽथ कालेभ्यः (चोर्र्. : कलभ्य चोद्.)
काल-कालान्तरेभ्यश् च सर्वतः
नमस् ते रुद्ररुद्रेभ्यः।
तृतीय-ब्रह्म ।

ओम् तत्-संयोगय विद्महे
हृशीकेशाय धीमहि (चोर्र्.: धीत्महे चोद्.)
तन् नो विष्णुः (चोर्र्.: विस्ņउ चोद्.) प्रचोदयात्।
चतुर्थब्रह्म ।

रोधकः सर्व-विद्यानाम्
देव-दानवाधिपति-महापुरुष
नमो ऽस्तु ते
पञ्चमब्रह्म।

shaiva inspiration

See panchopaniShan-mantras, and the 5 mantra-body TW.

The four Brahmas after the first are evidently modelled on the Saiva Brahmas in the order (1) Vamadeva (VĀMADEVAYA NAMO JYEṢTHAYA NAMO RUDRAYA NAMAH KĀLĀYA NAMAH KALAVIKARAṆAYA NAMO BALAVIKARAṆĀYA NAMO BALAPRA- MATHANAYA NAMAH SARVABHŪTADAMANĀYA NAMO MANONMANĀYA NAMAḤ), (2) Aghora (AGHOREBHYO ‘THA GHOREBHYO GHORAGHORATAREBHYAŚ CA SARVATAḤ SARVA SARVEBHYO NAMAS TE RUDRARŪPEBHYAḤ), (3) Tatpuruşa (TATPURUŞAYA VIDMAHE MAHĀDEVĀYA DHĪMAHI TAN NO RUDRAḤ PRACODAYĀT), and (4) Īśāna (ISĀNAḤ SARVAVIDYĀNĀM ĪŚVARAḤ SARVABHŪTĀNĀM BRAHMANO ‘DHIPATIR BRAHMA SIVO ME ‘STU SADA SIVAḤ). The first Brahma has nothing in common with the remaining Śaiva Brahma, that of Sadyojāta. - Sanderson.

Observation in last sentence is not correct in my opinion.
भवेभवे - नरेनरे
भजस्वमान्/भवस्वमान् - नर यस्मान्

Some appear to be replacements with later vocabulary aimed at “improvement”: tatpuruṣāya vs tatsaṁyogāya, īśānaḥ-sarvavidyānām vs rodhaka-sarvvavidyānām. Some appear to be hasty imitations with minor changes for the sake of it, and it doesn’t make much sense as the other: rudrarudrebhyaḥ is a replacement for rudrarūpebhyaḥ but imitates the repetition in ghoraghoratarebhyaḥ in the earlier half of the aghora.
- GA

Additionally I would add that there is even semantic intent: nare nare -> in man after man; bhave bhave -> in being after being/ life upon life.

narannAtha: the lord of the men who among men is the highest; adhibhave or atibhave into the primal being or into beyond being. Those also convey an approximately parallel sense.

Śaiva Pañcabrahmas are found in TA. More importantly, the aghora brahma is found right at the end of Śatarudriya in the Maitrāyaṇī Saṁhitā itself. There is the duplication of consonants in rodhaka-sarvvavidyānām, which is characteristic of the milieu and region where it was composed. - GA

मानसतरङ्गिणीकृत् - Variants

BTW there is the +ve variant of the Ishana equivalent that makes it more in line with the shiva version: bodhaka-sarvavidyAnAm rather than rodhaka which is used in abhichArika practice. I think @goghritaM found the former to be prevalent in Andhran practice?

Diwakar Acharya’s publication of the early pA~ncharAtra text, the aShTAdasha vidhAna has the variant bodhaka. An alternative reading mentioned to me goes “bodhaka ssarvavidyAnAM vyApin sarvabhUtAnAM…..namostute.
